Step-by-Step Process:

  • Step 1: Choose a simple strategy (e.g., SMA crossover)
  • Step 2: Download 5+ years of historical data
  • Step 3: Clean and prepare the data

  • Step 11: Connect to paper trading account
  • Step 12: Monitor for 1-3 months

Goal: Execute and refine strategies with real capital

Going Live Checklist:

  • Strategy backtested with realistic assumptions
  • Walk-forward validation passed
  • Paper trading successful for 3+ months
  • Risk controls implemented and tested
  • Monitoring and alert systems in place
  • Emergency procedures documented
  • Starting with 1-5% of total capital
  • Psychological readiness for losses

Risk Management Rules:

  • Never risk more than 1-2% per trade
  • Maximum portfolio exposure: 20-30%
  • Daily loss limit: 5% of capital
  • Monthly loss limit: 10% of capital
  • Correlation limits between strategies
  • Position size limits per asset
  • Leverage limits (avoid excessive leverage)

Performance Review Schedule:

  • Daily: Check open positions, P&L, alerts
  • Weekly: Review trade log, analyze winners/losers
  • Monthly: Full performance analysis, strategy comparison
  • Quarterly: Strategy optimization, portfolio rebalancing
  • Annually: Tax planning, major strategy overhaul

Common Pitfalls to Avoid:

  • Revenge trading after losses
  • Increasing position size after wins (overconfidence)
  • Not adapting to changing market conditions
  • Ignoring warning signs (drawdowns, low Sharpe)
  • Over-diversification (too many strategies)
  • Under-diversification (single strategy risk)
  • Emotional interference with system rules
  • Not keeping proper records

Signs to Stop Trading a Strategy:

  • Drawdown exceeds historical maximum by 50%
  • Sharpe ratio drops below 0.5 for 3+ months
  • Win rate significantly lower than backtest
  • Market conditions have fundamentally changed
  • Better strategies identified and validated
